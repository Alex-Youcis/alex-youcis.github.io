An integral comparison of crystalline and de Rham cohomology

Collaborators: Abhinandan
Status: Submitted
Tags: \(p\)-adic Hodge theory, prismatic \(F\)-gauges, crystalline cohomology, de Rham cohomology
Description
In this paper we give an integral comparison between twisted crystalline and twisted de Rham cohomology, appropriately defined. This makes use of an analogue of Dwork's trick in the stacky approach to prismatic cohomology, and allows for arbitrary prismatic coefficients. In the case of the structure sheaf, this rationally recovers Berthelot–Ogus's celebrated isomorphism. Finally, we make use of this integral comparison to study the relationship between torsion in crystalline and de Rham cohomology.

An integral analogue of Fontaine’s crystalline functor

Collaborators: Naoki Imai and Hiroki Kato
Status: Submitted
Tags: \(p\)-adic Hodge theory, prismatic \(F\)-gauges, Fontaine–Laffaille theory
Description
We develop an integral analogue of the functor \(D_{\mathrm{crys}}\), and relate it to Fontaine–Laffaille and Dieudonné theory. Notably, in the Fontaine–Laffaille range \([0,p-2]\), the usual integral \(p\)-adic Hodge objects (local systems, Fontaine–Laffaille modules, prismatic \(F\)-crystals, and prismatic \(F\)-gauges) agree. This clarifies the successes of Fontaine–Laffaille in that range and its limits outside it.

Canonical Integral Models of Shimura Varieties of Abelian Type

Collaborators: Patrick Daniels
Status: Forum of Mathematics, Sigma
Tags: Shimura varieties, integral models, \(p\)-adic Hodge theory, \(p\)-adic shtukas
Description
We prove a conjecture of Pappas–Rapoport for all abelian-type Shimura varieties with parahoric level at \(p>3\), showing Kisin–Pappas–Zhou integral models are canonical.

Collaborators: Naoki Imai and Hiroki Kato
Status: Submitted
Tags: Shimura varieties, integral models, \(p\)-adic Hodge theory, prismatic \(F\)-gauges, Fontaine–Laffaille theory
Description
We construct a prismatic \(F\)-gauge realization of the universal \(\mathcal{G}\)-motive on integral canonical models of abelian-type Shimura varieties (hyperspecial level), yielding new \(p\)-adic Hodge information and a Serre–Tate–type result.

Collaborators: Piotr Achinger and Marcin Lara
Status: To appear in American Journal of Mathematics
Tags: Rigid analytic geometry, covering space theory, geometric arcs, de Jong fundamental group
Description
