U of T number theory seminar (2025–26)

  Wednesday, 2:10–3:0X PM (50 minutes + questions)   Bahen Center, Room 6183   Organizers: Florian Herzig and Alex Youcis
Subscribe 2024–2025 NT seminar

About

This seminar features research talks in analytic and arithmetic aspects of number theory broadly construed (including \(p\)-adic Hodge theory, automorphic forms, Galois representations, and related topics). Talks target a general number theory audience at the University of Toronto and nearby institutions.

Information for speakers

Abstract

Please make the abstract no more than one paragraph. Please feel free to use TeX which will be rendered.

Format & length

50 minutes + short Q&A. Please make the first 20 minutes accessible to a number-theory-focused graduate student.

Technical logistics
  • If you are giving a slide talk, please send the slides three days in advance to alex.youcis@gmail.com.
  • The talk will be broadcast on Zoom.
  • The talk will be recorded and posted, unless requested otherwise.
Funding

Funding for travel and lodging is limited and not guaranteed; please contact alex.youcis@gmail.com for more details.

Call for speakers If you’d like to give a talk, send a title/abstract and preferred dates to alex.youcis@gmail.com; include "U of T NT seminar" in your email subject.
